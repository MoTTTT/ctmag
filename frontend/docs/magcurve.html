MAGCURVE TEST PROCEDURE:
 
  • The hardware must be set up as stipulated for the current transformer magnetising test. Turn the variac control knob to 0V. Ensure that the CT to be tested is demagnetised. Apply power to the variac. Select the "Start Magcurve Test" option on the application screen. In the statustextfield just below the menu-bar at the top of the screen, the voltage and current readings for the respective channels as measured by the Promet sensing unit will be displayed.
  • Increase the variac voltage at a constant tempo of 10V/sec (make use of the voltage dial on which the variac control knob is mounted as a reference in order to maintain a constant turning rate). When a kneepoint is detected on the up sweep of the variac, the application notifies the user by sounding a beep.
  • Stop turning the variac control knob i.e. stop increasing the applied voltage to the CT being tested.
  • Wait 5 seconds for the CT to settle before proceeding with the down sweep i.e. turning the variac control knob down to 0V.
  • Start the down sweep very slowly and increase the speed to a maximum of 10V/sec. Try to maintain a constant sweep at this rate, do not exceed this maximum speed.
  • A second beep will indicate that the end of test has been reached. The magnetisation curve for the CT under test would be drawn on the "CT Magnetisation Curve" panel. The measured kneepoint for this curve will be indicated as a voltage and current reading in the "MagCurve Results" panel assigned to the "main kneepoint".
  • This "maincurve" can now be assigned to one of the three phases by selecting "RED", "WHITE"  or "BLUE". To ignore this curve i.e. not assign it to one of the phases, select the "STOP TEST" button. By assigning the maincurve  to one of the phases, the curve is redrawn for the selected phase in the "3 Phase CT Magnetisation Curve" panel and its calculated kneepoint assigned to the appropriate phase in the "Mag Curve Results" panel.
CURVE CHARACTERISTICS:

The magnetisation effect in a CT has a settling time. The magnetising curves in the application are drawn on the down sweep of the variac since the measured results for a CT are inherently more stable on the down sweep. In tests conducted stable, accurate results were achieved when the up sweep was maintained at a constant rate of 10V/sec, followed by a waiting period of 5 seconds to allow the CT magnetisation effect to settle before proceeding with the down sweep. The down sweep is slowly increased to a maximum turning rate of 10V/sec.

Since settling time will differ from CT to CT, it is advisable to conduct a series of tests on a reference CT in order to achieve the correct, maximum continious sweep speed. The results for the tests conducted on the same CT should be identical. If the curves for the same CT do not match it is indicative of inconsistant and incorrect sweep speeds. Sweeps which are too fast would tend to flatten the drawn curve. If the angle of the magnetisation curve differ for the tests conducted on the same CT it is a clear indication of inconsistent sweep speeds between the different tests conducted. If there is a difference in the highest voltage measured it would indicate that the up sweeps were inconsistent, the up sweeps were too fast and/or not enough settling time were allowed. Differences in the maximum current measured indicate that the variac was not consistantly stopped at the sounding of the first beep which could be indicative of too fast sweep speeds. Slower sweep speeds are advised if results remain inconsistent.
