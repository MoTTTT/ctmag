BRIEF OVERVIEW/PROMET CALIBRATION UNIT:

CAPABILITIES:

This unit was developed for the Metering and Protection Industry to determine if Voltage Transformers and Current Transformers are within specifications.

This unit can do the following tests when connected to a personal computer,
 

  1. Magnetizing test of Current Transformers.
  2. Ratio test of Current Transformers.
  3. Polarity test of Current Transformers.
  4. Ratio test of Voltage Transformers.
  5. Polarity test of Voltage Transformers.

Please note that at all times the above tests must be carried out on apparatus, which are at a zero potential and is disconnected or isolated from any live power systems. A rotating plant shall not be regarded as dead until it is stationary or is being slowly rotated by means of barring gear while not excited.

The software that comes with the PROMET will plot the Magnetizing Curves, show the ratio of the Current Transformer and Voltage Transformer and it will do a polarity test of both the Voltage and Current Transformers. All the information is contained in a file, which can be overlaid onto previous test to be able to compare results. The information is also available in a text file format that can be imported into a spreadsheet such as or Lotus 123 or a word processing package such as Wordpro.

Unit calibration date and time will be stored in software. A NLA lab with the relevant software and hardware adjustments will do all calibrations.

 
TESTING PROCEDURES.

Current Transformer Magnetizing Test.

Equipment needed:

1 Personal Computer e.g. Notebook.

1 Voltage injection set.

1 PROMET CT & VT TESTER

Various test leads.

 Connect the PROMET power cable to the 220-volt supply; connect the RS232 serial communications cable between the PROMET serial port and the serial port on the Notebook. Start the software.

Connect the various leads as indicated in the hardware test documentation. When all connections have been made and the relevant CT information filled in, start the magnetizing curve test.

Turn the variac control knob slowly in steps of +/- 10 volt. The PROMET will pass the information to the Notebook.

Once the knee point has been reached the Notebook will notify the user. The knee point is reached when a 10 % rise in voltage results in a 50% rise in current of the CT (Current Transformer) circuit. Once the knee point has been reached and the CT saturated, slowly turn the variac to reduce the voltage.

The magnetizing curve will be plotted on the Notebook screen and can be assigned to specific phase. The plots can be stored as files in the current working directory and printed in report format.
 

Current Transformer Ratio & Polarity Test.

Equipment needed:

1 Personal Computer e.g. Notebook.

Current injection set.

1 PROMET CT & VT TESTER

Various test leads.

 Connect the PROMET power cable to the 220-volt supply; connect the RS232 serial communications cable between the PROMET serial port and the serial port on the Notebook. Start the software.

Connect the various leads as indicated in the hardware test documentation. When all connections have been made and the CT (Current Transformer) information has been filled in, start the Ratio & Polarity test.

NOTE: That the leads must be connected in the correct order, otherwise the unit will not give the correct information with regards to the ratio and polarity test.

Turn the variac control knob slowly till the primary current threshold (set to 20% of the transformer’s current rating) is reached. The PROMET will pass the information to the Notebook. Once the 20% threshold has been reached the Notebook will notify the user (the current ratio threshold is set in the options menu item of the settings menu).

The ratio & polarity information will now be shown on the Notebook screen. This information can be assigned to a specific phase, saved to a file and printed in report format.
 

Voltage Transformer Ratio & Polarity Test

Equipment needed.

1 Personal Computer e.g. Notebook.

1 Voltage injection set.

1 PROMET CT & VT Tester.

Various test leads.

 Connect the PROMET power cable to the 220-volt supply; connect the RS232 serial communications cable between the PROMET serial port and the serial port on the Notebook. Start the software.

Connect the various leads as indicated in the hardware test documentation. When all connections have been made and the VT (Voltage Transformer) information has been filled in, start the Ratio & Polarity test.

NOTE: That the leads must be connected in the correct order, otherwise the unit will not give the correct information with regards to the ratio and polarity test.

Turn the variac control knob slowly till 220 V is reached. The PROMET will pass the information to the Notebook.

The ratio & polarity information will now be shown on the Notebook screen. This information can be assigned to a specific phase, saved to a file and printed in report format.
